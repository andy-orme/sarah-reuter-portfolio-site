About me

Often producing images using her phone, Sarah Reuter’s work investigates how this infinitely accessible technology can be used as a means to create work and then expands upon it through other more tactile processes.

Born in 1992, graduated from BA (Hons) Photography at Falmouth University in 2015. Often producing images using her phone, Sarah Reuter’s work investigates how this infinitely accessible technology can be used as a means to create work and then expands upon it through other more tactile processes.

As camera phones have opened up the canon of photography in recent years to more people than ever before, our connection to the ‘print’ has altered also. With this in mind Reuter’s work combines the immediate nature of photography with the meditative process of needlework.

Using a documentary style coupled with a conceptual context, her images lie within the realms of social and institutional critique.

As well as being a practicing photographer, Reuter has also worked for L A Noble Gallery, London.
