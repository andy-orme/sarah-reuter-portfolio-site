For All We Know

This studio work explores the belief in our visual culture that a portrait can reveal a person’s soul. The subjects are photographed within an environment devoid of distraction and no interaction takes place between the photographer and her subjects.

The idea behind this being that the subject’s attention drifts away from the performance of being photographed, thus allowing the portrait to display a truer representation of their selves.

By playing the subjects 2 songs, one which draws upon happy memories and another which sends their minds to darker times, the viewer is left to wonder which is being played within each portrait. Ultimately the work highlights the fact that as viewers we look for meaning and understanding which truly cannot be found.
