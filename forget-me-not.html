Forget Me Not

An intimate series charting the decline of a grandmother lost to Alzheimer's. Shot candidly using a camera phone, the images explore the photograph as a means of therapy and processing difficult times, as well as the loss and preservation of memory.

The sharp contrast between the delicate threads of embroidery stitched upon the C-type photographic prints has had a strong emotional impact upon audiences viewing the work. They remind us that there is great beauty and fragility in the real world, which should be acknowledged and cherished when much of life is lived in the realms of the virtual one.

Each print it created using a finite amount of thread inherited from her grandmother and as such, although editioned, each print is one of a kind.
